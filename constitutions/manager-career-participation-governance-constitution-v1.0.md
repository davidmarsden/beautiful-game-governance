The Beautiful Game

Manager Career, Participation & Governance Constitution v1.0

The last human layer. The world already knows everything about how clubs recruit players and almost nothing about how clubs recruit, retain, reward, lose and replace managers. In a persistent world, managers are the scarcest resource. This document consolidates the manager rules scattered across the other constitutions and completes them — and it carries the rules that change the rules.

---

Design Philosophy

A manager is not a fixed point the world is built around. He is an asset with a career: appointed by agreement, judged against expectation, rewarded, courted, and — three different ways — lost. The manager lifecycle mirrors the player lifecycle already built, and reuses its machinery wherever it can rather than inventing new mechanics. Above all, no club ever sits empty, no manager is ever moved against his will, and no carefully built constitution is ever altered casually.

---

Constitutional Principles

The Manager Lifecycle Mirrors the Player Lifecycle — entry, valuation, approach, appointment, objectives, performance, reward, movement, exit, reappointment, legacy. Reuse, don't reinvent.

Consent, Always — Except Removal — a manager cannot be appointed, retained, moved or transferred without his consent. The only involuntary transitions are dismissal and removal. A manager's career is his to steer; only failure is done to him.

Managers Are Assets — they have a value, a market, and a cost to prise away — but never a cage.

Expectation, Not Position — a manager's job is judged relative to his Contract Objective, never raw league position. The same equal-boards fairness that governs Backing governs employment.

Three Ways To Lose A Job — relegation (sporting, automatic, season-end), lost confidence (the mid-season sack race), and abandonment (participation failure). Distinct tracks, distinct triggers.

Confidence Is Earned And Recoverable — board confidence is a live meter, not a one-way ratchet. A manager can climb out of the danger zone.

Quiet But Leaky — a board's search for a replacement is private, but surfaces through the same rumour rails as everything else. The chairman is the system, not an administrator.

Continuity Above All — a caretaker bridges every gap. No club is ever left dead.

The Rules That Change The Rules — the constitution can be amended, but only through a defined, tiered, transparent process, by a defined electorate.

Determinism & Transparency — every value, confidence level and trigger derives from logged events and published rules. No administrator sacks, appoints, or amends by discretion.

PART 1 — THE MANAGER LIFECYCLE

Entry → Appointment → Objective → Performance → Reward / Approach → Exit (relegation, lost confidence, or abandonment) → Caretaker → Reappointment → Career & Legacy.

Each stage below either consolidates a rule that already lives elsewhere (and is cross-referenced) or defines something new.

---

PART 2 — CONSENT, ENTRY AND APPOINTMENT

2.1 The Consent Rule

No manager is appointed to a club, retained by one, approached, or transferred between clubs without his consent. The sole exceptions are the involuntary exits — relegation, lost confidence, and abandonment removal — which by their nature are done to a manager, not agreed with him. In every other respect a manager's career is steered by the manager himself, exactly as a player's move requires the player's consent. The people of this world are moved by agreement, and only ever lose their place by failing in it.

2.2 Entry

New managers enter a shared pool with baseline Manager Reputation (Contracts §5) and a Home Knowledge Region (Youth Discovery). One manager controls one club; no multi-accounting (World §3).

2.3 Appointment

Vacancies are filled in a published priority order (consolidated from World §8, which now defers here for detail): managers moving from other clubs, then dismissed managers meeting published reappointment criteria, then new entrants. Appointment requires the manager's consent (2.1); no one is assigned a club against his will.

Every appointment sets a public Contract Objective drawn deterministically from the club's Expected Finish (World §7). The objective is the yardstick for everything that follows.

---

PART 3 — MANAGER VALUE AND THE APPROACH MARKET

Managers have no real-world transfer value to borrow, so the world computes one.

Manager Value (deterministic) =
BaseValue(Manager Reputation) × DivisionMultiplier × (1 + BuildingCreditFactor) × ContractFactor

- BaseValue rises with Reputation along a published curve.
- DivisionMultiplier rewards managing at a higher level.
- BuildingCreditFactor reflects a track record of improving clubs (Youth Discovery Building Credit).
- ContractFactor rises with years remaining — a manager deep into a long deal costs more to prise away.
All four are dials.

The Approach Mechanism — a club may approach a contracted manager. This reuses the enquiry machinery and the 72-Hour Response Rule (Information §1.3): an approach is an enquiry, and it must be answered.

- Compensation is paid to the current club = ManagerValue × ContractTimeRemainingFactor. A manager in his final months, or out of contract, is cheap or free — exactly as a player's fee collapses near contract end.
- The current club may demand compensation up to a published ceiling, and may reject offers below it — but it cannot veto a move once the ceiling is met and the manager wishes to go. A club's leverage is the fee, never a cage; an absolute block would trap successful managers and make "success pulls him up and out" hollow.
- The manager's consent is always required and always sufficient to decline: clubs may agree a move he can still refuse, but they can never force one on him, nor forbid one he has decided for himself once the ceiling is met.
- Approaches accrue Interest Score and leak as rumours (Information Part 3): "Division 1 club understood to be monitoring the Division 4 manager." Interest leaks; compensation figures and the sealed terms do not.

Symmetry with relegation — relegation forces a manager down and out; success pulls him up and out. Being approached is the constitutional reward for success, and the club's counter-move is the extension offer (Part 4). The tension between a club fighting to keep a manager and a bigger club coming for him is the dramatic engine, and neither side can be automatic.

---

PART 4 — OBJECTIVES, PERFORMANCE AND REWARD

Performance is measured against the Contract Objective (World §7), relative to expectation. Reward flows through the existing economy, not a parallel one.

Promotion triggers, as the club's move:
- an Extension Offer (one additional year) — an offer the manager may accept or decline (2.1), never an automatic lock that traps a manager who wishes to move;
- an Objective Review — the objective is re-set to the club's new level.

Promotion does not pay a separate bonus. Over-delivery against objective already earns Board Backing under Scouting & Finance §8; routing a second reward through this document would double-count the same achievement. The novel, non-duplicative parts of success are the extension offer and the objective review.

---

PART 5 — BOARD CONFIDENCE AND THE SACK RACE

Board Backing rewards what a manager has achieved. Board Confidence reflects where he is heading. The two share the expectation-relative inputs but answer different questions — "how much may I spend?" and "will I keep my job?" A manager can be living off past achievement while his trajectory collapses. That is the manager in a slump.

Board Confidence (0–100) moves toward a target set by recent, expectation-relative performance:

PerformanceGap = ActualPace − ObjectivePace

where ObjectivePace is the points/position trajectory implied by the Contract Objective and ActualPace is the current one, recency-weighted. Participation (Information §1.2) and conduct — press stances (Information §2.2) and kept or broken promises (Contracts) — modulate it. All weights are dials.

Because the measure is expectation-relative, a minnow sitting mid-table is secure (he is beating "avoid relegation") while a giant sitting fourth is in danger (he is missing "title challenge"). A manager is never sacked for league position — only for position relative to what was promised. This is the equal-boards philosophy applied to employment.

Escalation — published, deterministic stages on declining Confidence:

Confidence| Stage| What happens
≥ 60| Secure| Normal operation.
40–59| Concern| Private. The manager sees his standing slipping; no external sign.
25–39| Quiet Search| The board discreetly identifies replacement candidates. This generates Interest Score on other managers (Part 3), which begins to leak as rumours. The incumbent reads the writing on the wall.
10–24| Public Speculation| The press openly question the manager's future; rumour tier high.
Vote of Confidence| Trigger| Crossing the critical threshold (below ~10, or a sharp negative shock) auto-issues the chairman's public statement of "full backing." Constitutionally this is the kiss of death: it opens a fixed grace window of N matches.
0, or window expiry| Dismissal| If results do not recover above the recovery threshold within the window — or Confidence reaches zero — the manager is dismissed mid-season.

Confidence is recoverable at every stage before dismissal. A winning run against objective lifts it and a manager can escape the sack race entirely; the vote-of-confidence window is the one explicitly make-or-break phase. The chairman is the deterministic system, never an administrator; his statements are generated narrative grounded in the Confidence state (Information §2.1, narrate-never-adjudicate). N, the thresholds and the recovery bar are dials.

The aftermath of a sacking — a mid-season dismissal does not condemn a club to limp through the season under a stand-in. The vacancy is filled immediately if a manager is available under the Part 2 priority order; only if none is available does a caretaker hold the club, until one is (Part 6). The same applies to any mid-season vacancy however it arises.

PART 6 — PARTICIPATION AND THE CARETAKER

The soft consequences of poor participation already exist (Information §1.2: Professionalism feeds reputation and attractiveness). This is the hard layer, and it is a different failure from the sack race: the sack race is failing on the pitch; this is not turning up at all.

Minimum standards: submit a team, answer transfer communications within 72 hours, maintain valid contact information, register a squad. Away Mode (Information §1.2) is the legitimate way to be absent and is never a breach.

Escalation: Warning → Caretaker Status → Removal. Each stage triggers at a published threshold of missed obligations within a window. The numbers are dials; that the thresholds are published and fixed is not, so the path from neglect to removal is always deterministic and never a judgement call.

The Caretaker is the continuity mechanism the world has lacked. When a manager abandons his post, is dismissed mid-season (Part 5), or moves on (Part 3), the club is run by a caretaker on the manager's published defaults — auto-team, auto-responses, no transfer activity. The caretaker is a bridge, not a sentence: a substantive replacement is appointed as soon as one is available under the Part 2 priority order, and the caretaker runs the club only until then — which may be at once, or may last to season's end if no manager is available. No club is ever left dead, and no rival ever gains from a neighbour's empty chair.

---

PART 7 — EXIT AND REAPPOINTMENT

Four ways out: relegation (automatic, season-end), lost confidence (mid-season, Part 5), abandonment (participation, Part 6), and voluntary exit (resignation or an accepted approach, Part 3). The first three are involuntary and need no consent; the fourth is the manager's own choice (2.1).

On any exit: Building Credit resets (Youth Discovery). Manager Reputation, Knowledge Regions and the full career record persist. A departed manager re-enters the pool and may be reappointed under the Part 2 priority order — with his consent — beginning a fresh tenure.

---

PART 8 — CAREER AND LEGACY

A manager's career is the largest missing thread of the legacy that runs through the whole project — fanbase, reputation, trophies, development reputation, building credit, stability. The world records, permanently: clubs managed, promotions and relegations, trophies, building history, peak reputation, and length of service. This record persists across every move and dismissal, feeds Manager Value (Part 3) and an approaching club's interest, and is the reason a Season 1 decision still matters in Season 25.

Because the record is public and legible, managers acquire social identities the world reads at a glance — the builder, the job-hopper, the miracle worker, the relegation fireman, the communicator, the ghost, the cup specialist. None is a label the game assigns; each is the visible shape of a career, emerging from the same record that feeds value and interest. That legibility — knowing what a manager is by what he has done — is the persistent-world magic the whole project chases. There is no separate Legacy Constitution; legacy is connective tissue, and the manager career is its spine.

PART 9 — GOVERNANCE: THE RULES THAT CHANGE THE RULES

Everything else in the project is highly constitutional; the amendment process must be too, or years of careful construction can be undone casually. Change runs through three tiers:

Tier| Scope| Threshold
Ordinary Rule Change| Operational dials and numbers (the values marked "dial" throughout)| Administration proposes; Community Council consults; lightweight.
Constitutional Change| Structural rules and principles| Voting Manager supermajority, with notice and a published rationale.
Emergency Change| Live exploits or breakage threatening the world| Administration may act immediately and time-limited, but the change must be ratified afterward under the appropriate tier or it lapses.

The electorate is defined precisely. A Voting Manager is a manager currently appointed to a club and not under suspension or caretaker-removal. This includes the newly appointed; it excludes caretakers, unattached managers awaiting reappointment, the suspended, and any manager already in the removal process. (A minimum tenure before enfranchisement is an available anti-abuse dial, off by default.)

The Community Council represents the Voting Managers between votes. Administration administers and holds only the emergency power, always subject to ratification. This resolves the standing rule that administration is bound by the published rules: the amendment process is the single legitimate channel through which those rules change. Every amendment is logged and versioned, exactly as these constitutions already are.

Golden Rule

A manager is hired by agreement, judged against expectation, and kept only while the board believes in where he is going. He can be relegated out, abandoned out, or lost-confidence out — but never sacked for sitting where his club was always expected to sit, never moved against his will, and never caged when a bigger club meets the price and he wants to go. Success makes him wanted; failure makes him vulnerable; absence makes him replaceable. No chair stays empty, no rival profits from an empty chair, and not even these rules change except by the managers whose world it is.
