The Beautiful Game

Youth Discovery & Recruitment Constitution v6.1

---

Design Philosophy

Real football rewards scouting, judgement and patience.

The objective is not to provide equal outcomes.

The objective is to provide equal opportunity.

The Beautiful Game is built on a simple principle:

«A manager who understands football should always be capable of outperforming a manager with greater financial resources.»

Money may expand reach. Money may improve infrastructure. Money may increase attractiveness. Money may not replace judgement.

The competitive advantage comes from identifying future stars before the wider market recognises them.

---

Constitutional Principles

Football Knowledge Before Financial Power — money widens reach; knowledge creates opportunity; judgement creates success. A manager who identifies tomorrow's superstar before everyone else should always possess an advantage over a manager who merely waits for today's to become obvious.

Knowledge Is Public, Attention Is Private — football information belongs to everyone. No manager may purchase exclusive access to reality. Advantage comes from where managers choose to look, how much attention they pay, and what conclusions they draw.

Scouting Provides Confidence, Not Truth — scouting may narrow uncertainty; it may not reveal certainty. Two managers may evaluate the same player and reach different conclusions. This is intentional.

Commitment Is Rewarded, Mediocrity Is Not — the game favours managers who build successful long-term projects. Time served alone earns nothing. Only successful construction of a club creates durable advantage.

Stability Creates Advantage — the game rewards sustained attention. Managers who remain committed to regions, clubs and long-term projects gradually accumulate advantages that cannot be purchased instantly. Rapid change remains possible; it is rarely optimal.

---

1. The Governing Rule

Three things are never hidden.

Existence — every real player exists in the game world the moment they enter the database. No player is hidden or locked behind a scouting system. Any manager may pursue any player at any time.

Current Ability — public, calculated using the Player Rating Constitution. Any manager can understand exactly why a player holds a given rating. No hidden Ability values exist.

History — club history, international history, appearances, goals, assists, clean sheets, market value history and position history are all public. Managers are never denied factual information.

---

2. The Unknown

Only one thing is uncertain.

Potential represents future footballing ceiling. It cannot be known with certainty because future football cannot be known with certainty. Every recruitment decision is a prediction.

«How good will this player eventually become?»

The official Potential Band is public and computable via the Player Rating Constitution. Every manager sees the same band. What nobody can know is where inside it the player will land. Managers compete through judgement, not information access.

---

3. Knowledge Regions

Managers begin with a fixed number of Knowledge Region slots.

Prototype: 3 slots per manager.

The slot count is a global constant. It cannot be purchased or expanded through wealth, club size or progression. Specialisation must remain scarce.

Region Selection

Examples: England, Germany, France, Netherlands, Scandinavia, South America, Africa, Japan, North America.

Within their regions managers receive faster discovery alerts, better contextual information, and more confident assessments of Potential.

Regional Depth

Depth is tracked as Effective Seasons. Each season held contributes (1 + Learning Bonus) effective seasons.

Level| Effective Seasons| Clarity Multiplier
1| 0| 1.00
2| ≥ 1| 0.90
3| ≥ 2| 0.80
4| ≥ 4| 0.70
5| ≥ 6| 0.60

Managers can view both Current Level and Effective Seasons.

Example: South America — Level 4 — 4.8 Effective Seasons.

Higher levels provide greater confidence. They never provide certainty.

---

4. Building Credit

Football knowledge persists. Building momentum does not. Building Credit is earned at a manager's current club.

Building Season — achieved if any of: top-half league finish, improved division-adjusted standing, promotion, continental qualification, a published cup-success threshold, or a major trophy. Promotion always qualifies regardless of any other factor. Earns +1 Building Credit. Maximum 1 per season.

Flat Season — survival with no significant improvement, no major success and no relegation. Earns 0 Building Credit. The tenure streak continues.

Failure Season — any season ending in relegation. Relegation automatically triggers dismissal under the World Constitution; there is no distinction between relegation and dismissal. A Failure Season ends the tenure streak and resets Building Credit.

Cross-Division Comparison

Improvement is measured using Pyramid Rank (lower is better):

Pyramid Rank = (Division Tier − 1) × Clubs Per Division + League Position

Example: Division 1, 15th = Rank 15; Division 2, 1st = Rank 21; Division 2, 10th = Rank 30. This evaluates promotion and consolidation fairly across divisions.

Learning Bonus

Building Credit| Learning Bonus
0–2| 0%
3–5| 5%
6–10| 10%
11+| 15%

The Learning Bonus accelerates Regional Depth accumulation.

Persistence

Knowledge Regions and Regional Depth belong to the manager and survive club changes, resignations and dismissals. Building Credit belongs to the current club tenure and resets whenever that tenure ends. Football knowledge is transferable; club-building momentum is not.

Region Replacement

Managers may replace regions between seasons. Replacing a region removes its accumulated Effective Seasons and resets it to Level 1. All other regions remain unchanged. Knowledge is earned through football time; it is never purchased.

---

5. Scouting Networks

Scouting supplements knowledge. It does not replace it. Scouting provides Reach, Coverage and Clarity.

Clarity Factor

Assessment Width = Official Width × ClarityFactor

ClarityFactor = max(0.40, RegionFactor × ScoutingFactor)

RegionFactor — the Knowledge Region multiplier if the player is in one of the manager's regions; otherwise 1.00.

ScoutingFactor

Level| Factor
None| 1.00
Basic| 0.90
Good| 0.82
Strong| 0.74
Elite| 0.65

Scouting Levels are defined in the Scouting & Finance Constitution.

Precision Floor — ClarityFactor ≥ 0.40. The future remains unknowable.

---

6. Discovery Pools

Discovery Pools organise information. They do not govern access. Any manager may pursue any player regardless of pool membership.

---

7. Discovery Reports

Public Facts (identical for every manager): Name, Position, Club, Nationality, Age, Current Ability, Official Potential Band.

Manager Assessment — a narrower band centred on the same midpoint. Managers gain confidence, not truth.

---

8. Competition & Profile

No hidden awareness system exists. Competition emerges from public information. Profile is an optional public indicator of visibility; it measures attention, not talent.

---

9. Recruitment Windows

Multiple interested clubs trigger a Recruitment Window. There is no first-click advantage. Success depends on financial package, club attractiveness, playing opportunity, manager reputation and player preferences.

---

10. Player Choice

Players are not assets. Players make decisions. They may reject the highest wage, the largest transfer fee, or the most prestigious club. Human behaviour is part of football.

---

11. Development Pathways

Young players value first-team football, development opportunities, clear progression routes and manager trust. Smaller clubs therefore possess genuine recruitment advantages.

---

12. Recruitment Events

Triggered automatically by published thresholds. No administrator discretion exists. Events apply only to available players. Ownership is never overridden. Real-world fame does not create redistribution.

---

13. Compensation & Sell-On Rights

All compensation mechanisms must be formula-based, deterministic, public and reproducible.

Sell-On Clauses — defined contractually, distributed automatically.

Development Levy — trigger: any in-world transfer before age 24. Formula: 5% × Real-World Market Value at the date of transfer.

Free Transfers — the levy applies to any under-24 move, including a free transfer on contract expiry. Because it is keyed to real-world market value rather than a fee, it still computes when no fee exists; in that case the ACQUIRING club pays the levy directly to Developing Owners, subject to the minimum threshold and lifetime cap. (This matches the Contracts, Agents, Ambition & Club Attractiveness Constitution §1.)

Minimum Threshold — the levy is ignored below a published minimum market value (prototype: €100,000).

Developing Owners — a club qualifies if it held the player at least one full season while he was under 21.

Distribution: Share_i = (DevSeasons_i × RecencyWeight_i) ÷ Σ(DevSeasons × RecencyWeight), where RecencyWeight = 1 ÷ (Seasons Since Sale + 1).

Lifetime Cap — 30% × Highest Pre-24 Market Value. Prevents transfer-chain exploitation.

---

14. The Information Economy

The most valuable resource in The Beautiful Game is information. Managers gain advantage by watching football, understanding leagues, spotting trends, evaluating risk and predicting development. The market rewards conviction before consensus.

---

15. Attention Is Finite

No manager can perfectly follow world football. Attention is scarce. Choosing what to ignore is as important as choosing what to pursue. Opportunity emerges from how attention is allocated.

---

Relationship to Other Constitutions

The Player Rating Constitution defines Ability, Potential, Form and Reputation.

The Contracts, Agents, Ambition & Club Attractiveness Constitution defines contracts, agents, player preferences, club attractiveness, manager reputation, promises and transfer evaluation — how players, agents, managers and clubs interact once recruitment interest exists.

The World Constitution defines ownership, squad limits, financial controls, anti-hoarding rules, transfer-fee controls, and the dismissal and reappointment machinery.

The Scouting & Finance Constitution defines Scouting Levels, scouting costs and scout quality.

This document supersedes the former Draft system as the game's talent-allocation mechanism.

---

Golden Rule

Football knowledge is the primary competitive advantage in the game.

Managers should win because they understand football better than their rivals — not because they possess hidden information, superior spending power, or faster reactions. Longevity earns advantage only when it is built upon success.
