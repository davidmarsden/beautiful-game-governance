The Beautiful Game

Information, Media & Communication Constitution v1.2

The information-flow layer. Football knowledge is the currency of the game; this is where that currency is created, shared, distorted and exploited. It is not a peripheral system — it is the bridge that connects scouting, rumours, professionalism, reputation, attractiveness, stability, fan and board confidence, and recruitment. In real football, information touches all of these. So does this document.

---

Design Philosophy

A football world should never feel silent, should never be won by clicking, and should never speak in only one language. Information moves around the world by published rule: managers earn standing by turning up and engaging, not by being online; what is private stays private until a manager acts; the story of every match is always told — in every language — grounded in what actually happened; and it is told by identifiable voices, not an anonymous system.

---

Constitutional Principles

Information Is The Currency — the creation, flow and distortion of information is the central system, not a decoration on top of the game.

Reward Engagement, Not Presence — meeting the obligations of running a club matters; logging in does not. Clicking is never rewarded.

The World Is Never Silent — the game generates a full news and press layer automatically; humans may override it, but its absence never leaves the world empty.

One World, Every Language — every report, question and rumour is generated in each reader's own language. Translation is not a feature; it is a constitutional guarantee, because a global community should never be divided by the language its players happen to speak.

Identifiable Voices, Not An Anonymous System — news and questions originate from named media archetypes with consistent perspectives, so the world feels reported rather than computed.

Narrate, Never Adjudicate — generated media describes the deterministic facts. It never invents events, never changes game state, never leaks secrets, and never knows more than the public is allowed to know. Prose has no mechanical power. The press describes; the game decides — and that boundary protects the entire project.

Leaks Reveal Interest, Not Offers — rumours expose that interest exists and how strong it is, never what anyone bid. Sealed-bid recruitment is preserved.

Reactions Are States, Not Rewrites — interest unsettles a player through a transient state layered on his fixed personality, never by editing his committed preferences.

Determinism & Transparency — every score, rumour and effect derives from logged events and published rules. No administrator discretion.

PART 1 — MANAGER PROFESSIONALISM AND RESPONSIVENESS

1.1 Activity, Not Presence

Logins are never measured or rewarded. What counts is whether a manager meets the obligations of running a club: submitting a team, handling contracts, responding to approaches, registering his squad, attending press obligations. A manager who logs in fifty times gains nothing; a manager who turns up once and does what is required loses nothing.

1.2 Manager Professionalism (public score)

Professionalism = round(100 × Obligations Met ÷ Obligations Due) over a rolling window.

Each obligation is scored BINARY — met within its window, or not. Speed beyond the window earns nothing: responding in two minutes is no better than responding in twenty hours. This is deliberate — the moment faster beats on-time, the login race returns through the back door.

Obligations include: team submission, contract handling, transfer responsiveness (1.3), squad registration, press participation (Part 2).

Effects (bounded): Professionalism feeds Manager Reputation and Club Stability (Contracts Constitution §4–5), player confidence and club attractiveness. A manager who disappears for weeks becomes less attractive — not because the game punishes him, but because footballers prefer a club run by someone who turns up. Professionalism never affects match results.

Away Mode — a manager may flag a published, time-limited absence. Obligations during Away Mode are excused (no Professionalism penalty); the club runs on the manager's published defaults (auto-team, auto-response). Real life is never punished. Windows are generous and measured in absolute time, so no time zone is disadvantaged.

1.3 The 72-Hour Response Rule

Every transfer approach or enquiry requires a response within 72 hours. Valid responses: Accept, Reject, Interested, Considering, Counter-offer. There is no obligation to negotiate — only to respond.

- Silence → the approach lapses (a soft reject; the enquiring club is freed) and the silent manager takes one missed obligation against Professionalism.
- Spam protection — a manager may mark a player Not For Sale, which auto-responds Reject and suppresses repeat-enquiry obligations. Duplicate approaches from the same club within a window create no new obligation.
- This rule governs the enquiry and negotiation layer only. Formal bids inside a Recruitment Window still resolve sealed, at window close (Youth Discovery §9) — they are not per-message obligations.
- Repeated, sustained failure is not handled here as a soft ding alone. Once it crosses into abandonment, it escalates — warning, caretaker status, removal — under the Manager Career, Participation & Governance Constitution (Part 6). That is what prevents abandoned clubs becoming permanent transfer black holes; this document owns the soft signal, that one owns the employment consequence.

1.4 External Communication

The game builds no chat or messaging. Communication lives where managers already are — WhatsApp, Discord, Telegram, social platforms. The game provides only identity and expectation: each profile carries Preferred Contact Method, Time Zone, Language(s) and Typical Response Time. These are informational, setting expectations between managers; the 72-hour rule is the only mechanical obligation.

1.5 Public Manager Metrics

A manager's standing is visible to everyone, because visibility is what creates the social norm the community actually wants — "this one always responds." Every manager carries a public strip:

Metric| Source
Reputation| Contracts §5
Professionalism| 1.2
Promise Fulfilment| Contracts (promise record)
Current Tenure| Manager Career Constitution

These are already-computed public values; displaying them together is the point. Managers learn quickly who turns up and who does not, and that reputation travels with a manager between clubs.

PART 2 — WORLD NEWS AND PRESS

2.1 Automatic News Generation

Every match and round generates, automatically and before any human writes: a match report, player ratings (from the deterministic Ability and Form), manager quotes (drawn from press stances, 2.2), and a league roundup.

- Grounded — generation describes only the deterministic facts: scores, events, ratings, standings, rumour tiers. It never invents an event, never alters game state, and never exposes anything the public information rules keep private. Narrate, never adjudicate.
- Multilingual — content is generated in each reader's chosen language (the One World, Every Language guarantee). A Portuguese manager gives a press conference in Portuguese; an English manager reads it in English. This removes one of the largest hidden barriers to participation in a global community.
- Editorial — managers may ignore, edit, replace or expand any piece about their own club. The generated version stands if they do nothing, so the world is never empty.

2.2 Press Conferences (mandatory participation, not writing)

Before and after each match the manager faces reporter questions — posed by a media archetype (Part 6) and generated deterministically from context: form, results, league position, rivalries, live transfer rumours. The manager does not write; he chooses a Stance.

Stance| Typical Effect
Confident| Fan Confidence +; Board Confidence + if results back it, − if they contradict it; raises expectation. High risk.
Defensive| Fan Confidence − slight; Board Confidence neutral; lowers pressure. Low risk.
Diplomatic| Fan/Board neutral to + slight; defuses Rivalry Heat. Safe.
Aggressive| Fan Confidence + (galvanises); Board Confidence − slight; inflames Rivalry Heat, especially toward a named rival. High risk.
Neutral / No comment| No effect. The default when a manager does not participate.

Effects are published and deterministic, modulated by context (Confident after a defeat is bolder, so swings are larger), exactly like the promise system. They move Fan Confidence (matchday attendance and revenue, Scouting & Finance §1), Board Confidence (Club Stability and the sack race, Manager Career Constitution §5) and Rivalry Heat. They never affect match results. Magnitudes are dials.

Participation is an obligation (feeds Professionalism). Non-participation defaults to Neutral plus a small Professionalism ding — never a heavy penalty.

2.3 Manager Personality (emergent)

Repeated stances build a public, emergent personality that informs the Manager Archetypes of the Contracts Constitution (consistently Aggressive → a combative identity, and so on). It emerges from conduct and is never assigned — and it feeds back into which reporters seek a manager out (Part 6).

PART 3 — INFORMATION LEAKAGE (TRANSFER RUMOURS)

3.1 Interest Score

For each club–player pair, an Interest Score accrues from logged actions:

Action| Points
Shortlisted| 1
Scout report requested| 2
Enquiry made| 5
Bid submitted| 10
Repeat bid| +5

The score decays over time (prototype: −1 per in-game week) so stale interest fades.

3.2 Rumour Tiers

Interest Score (per player, aggregated across interested clubs)| Public Outcome
0–4| No rumour — private
5–9| Local rumour
10–19| Press speculation
20+| Major story

Because shortlisting scores only 1, bulk private shortlisting never crosses the threshold — it leaks nothing. Only concrete actions (enquiries, bids) surface. This solves the flood problem at the root: managers can shortlist in bursts with no public noise.

3.3 What Leaks, and What Does Not

A rumour reveals that interest exists and how intense it is — never an offer's value. "Sources suggest Hamburg are monitoring João Silva" → "talks are understood to be underway" → "a major move is expected." The qualitative tier rises; the sealed-bid amounts stay sealed. You learn that rivals are circling, which raises competition and price-awareness, but you still bid blind.

3.4 Aggregation (anti-flood)

Per club, simultaneous interests are aggregated into a single roundup ("Club X is active in the market") rather than one story per player. A player gets his own story only when he individually crosses the speculation or major tier. A player's rumour updates in place as his tier changes; it is never re-posted per action.

3.5 Player and Agent Reactions (transient state)

Rising aggregate interest places the player in an Unsettled state — a transient modifier layered on his fixed personality, never an edit to his committed preference weights (Contracts §3). The state decays as interest cools, and the player resettles to baseline. It is to personality what Form is to Ability.

While Unsettled:

- His agent raises demands — "My client is attracting significant interest; we would like to discuss improved terms" — creating renewal pressure on the owning manager.
- His openness rises — his effective willingness to listen to offers increases slightly (a temporary shift in his Acceptance behaviour), without changing what he fundamentally values.
- His Ability and market value do NOT move. Both are reality-synced (Player Rating Constitution); rumours never touch them. Interest changes behaviour, not quality.

The owning manager has real choices, all with costs: improve terms (resolves the state, spends wage budget or Board Backing), sell (cash in at a strong moment), or hold (and carry the continued unsettlement). That is the pressure football managers actually face.

PART 4 — JOURNALISTS AND MEDIA PERSPECTIVES

The world's news and questions come from identifiable reporters, so it reads as reported rather than computed. Reporters are system actors — never human, never anonymous — and, crucially, never informed beyond the public.

4.1 Reporter Archetypes

Archetype| Focus| Drawn toward
Transfer Specialist| Targets, contracts, departures| Clubs active in the market; Unsettled players; expiring contracts
Tactical Analyst| Results, form, performance| Streaks, big matches, tactical questions
Local Reporter| Club, fans, community, loyalty| Fan-confidence shifts and hometown narratives; sympathetic in tone
Tabloid| Conflict, rivalry, pressure| The sack race, the vote of confidence, Aggressive managers, rivalries; sensational
Youth Correspondent| Academy and development| Youth Discovery activity, development reputation, breakthrough youngsters
League Desk| The competition, not the club| Title, promotion, relegation and (if introduced) playoff races; the bird's-eye view

Five archetypes are club-centric; the League Desk is competition-centric — the bird's-eye voice that asks "three wins from three; are Southall genuine promotion contenders?" rather than "your striker scored twice." The roster is a dial; further voices may be added.

4.2 How Reporters Are Chosen

Selection is deterministic and contextual. Each archetype carries a relevance score computed from the club's current situation, and the most relevant voices appear. A club fighting the drop and a manager throwing Aggressive stances pull the Tabloid forward; a club active in a recruitment window pulls the Transfer Specialist; an academy-heavy side pulls the Youth Correspondent; a club caught in a title, promotion or relegation race pulls the League Desk. The manager's emergent personality (2.3) shifts the weights. Nothing is assigned by hand.

4.3 What Reporters Can and Cannot Know

A reporter is a perspective over public facts, not an agent with private information. Reporters foreground different already-public facts — results, rumour tiers, confidence stages, ratings, standings — from an angle. They never possess or reveal a sealed bid, a hidden preference weight, or any other private state, because the system exposes none of it to anyone. This is the guard that keeps the press layer from becoming a leak: a Tabloid cannot print what the game itself does not know.

4.4 Continuity

Reporters follow threads drawn from the logged record, which gives the impression of journalists who remember. The manager who keeps insulting rivals keeps facing rivalry questions; the youth developer keeps facing academy questions; the manager in freefall keeps facing his future. Continuity is computed from history, so it is deterministic and reproducible — and it is one more place the project's "history matters" thread surfaces.

4.5 Voice and Language

Each reporter's perspective renders in each reader's own language. The Tabloid is sensational in Portuguese and in English alike; the voice is the archetype, not the tongue. A reporter archetype may modulate a press-conference stance's published effect within bounds — an Aggressive answer to a Tabloid rivalry question inflames a little more — but always inside the deterministic effect table of 2.2, never beyond it.

PART 5 — THE RECORDS LEDGER

A persistent world earns its weight from memory. The Records Ledger is the permanent, public register of the marks a world sets — the place where a run that ended becomes a record that stands, until one day it does not.

5.1 What It Holds

Records are kept at three scopes: club (longest unbeaten run, biggest win, most goals in a season, longest spell in the top flight), competition (title sequences, points records, promotion and relegation feats), and manager (longest tenure, most trophies, fastest promotion, best over-performance against expectation). The catalogue of recorded marks is a dial; the principle is that the world remembers across all three.

5.2 Breaking and Standing

A record is held until a result beats it. Breaking one is a deterministic, automatically detected event — the ledger records the new mark from the result the moment it happens, with no judgement involved. The news layer then tells the story (Part 2), narrating what the ledger has recorded: narrate, never adjudicate. The ledger holds the fact; the press supplies the drama.

A standing record accrues weight the longer it survives — a mark untouched for twelve seasons is treated, by the news layer, as more storied than one set last year, and its eventual fall is a bigger story. This weight is purely narrative. A record never confers a mechanical advantage and never feeds back into any game value; it is history, and history is told, not played.

5.3 Where Records Come From

The match engine produces the runs and results; this ledger remembers them; the news layer tells them — in every language, so a club's history belongs to its whole community and not only to those who can read about it in English. Records are the surfaced form of the project's "history matters" thread: the reason a Season 1 mark can still cast a shadow in Season 25, and the reason the League Desk has something to chase.

Relationship to Other Constitutions

Draws Ability and Form for reports, and the reality-synced value rumours must never move, from the Player Rating Constitution. Preserves the sealed-bid Recruitment Windows and consumes scouting and shortlist actions from the Youth Discovery & Recruitment Constitution. Feeds Manager Reputation, Club Stability, attractiveness, the Manager Archetypes and the fixed-personality / Unsettled-state model of the Contracts Constitution. Feeds Fan Confidence into matchday revenue (Scouting & Finance §1). Supplies Board Confidence, the rumour rails for manager approaches, and the soft participation signal to the Manager Career, Participation & Governance Constitution — which owns the hard consequences (caretaker, removal) this document deliberately does not. Takes results and runs from the Match Engine Constitution into the Records Ledger, and the career records it keeps sit alongside the manager legacy the Manager Career Constitution records. Operates under the governance and conduct rules of the World Constitution.

---

Golden Rule

The world is never silent, never solvable, never won by clicking, and never mute in any language.

Information flows by published rule. Managers earn standing by engaging, not by being present, and that standing is visible to all. What is private stays private until a manager acts, and even then only the interest leaks, never the offer. The story is always told — in every language, by named voices that know only what the world already knows — grounded in what truly happened. The only uncertainty it creates is the uncertainty of other managers, never of dice.
