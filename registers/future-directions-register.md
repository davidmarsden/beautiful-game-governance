The Beautiful Game

Future Directions Register

A single home for ideas raised during drafting and deliberately set aside — so they are neither lost nor prematurely built. Nothing here is a commitment; each is a parked possibility. An item graduates into its home constitution, or into a new document, when its time comes. Each entry names what it is, where it would live, why it waits, and the socket the architecture has already left for it.

MATCH ENGINE

Partnerships (Partnership Familiarity)
A third relationship state beside Squad Cohesion and Tactical Familiarity, for specific pairings rather than the whole squad: centre-back partnerships, striker partnerships, full-back-and-winger flanks, midfield triangles. A side can have high overall cohesion yet still lose a key partnership.
Socket: sits beside Cohesion and Familiarity in Part 3, and is expressed under Variance Before Mean — an established pairing should make disasters rarer, not every match brilliant.
Waits for: a later implementation appendix; not yet worth its own principle.

Specialisation versus Versatility
Versatility trades ceiling for flexibility. A genuine three-position player is invaluable across a congested season but may never reach a specialist's peak in any one role.
Socket: the Variance Before Mean principle and Role Fit — a specialist's edge reads as a higher mean in his one role, a versatile player's as lower variance across many.
Waits for: a player-attribute pass; the architecture already points at it.

Formation Families (its own document)
The catalogue of underlying structures — back three, back four, single pivot, double pivot, two-striker, wide-forward — and the soft-counter matrix defined over them.
Socket: Part 5 already mandates that balance is judged at the family level, not the individual formation; this is the catalogue and matrix that fills that mandate.
Waits for: the move from constitution to implementation; balancing families is a tuning project, not a principle.

Set Pieces
A central, decisive feature of real football, not yet modelled — potentially a route to goal, a sub-route, or a layer of its own.
Socket: Appendix A.4 (Route to Goal) notes the deliberate omission; it would extend that axis.
Waits for: a careful, late pass. Flagged a known rabbit hole: easy to let it swallow the model, so kept out until the rest is stable.

Deserved-Performance Decomposition
The likely successor to "ExpGoals is the deserved score" — surrounding xG with Territorial Control, Chance Quality and Tactical Execution, so the engine can recognise a plan well executed even when the xG was lost.
Socket: Appendix D.5 logs the direction and the discipline.
Waits for: real need, and then narrative first — any such dimension must become something the news layer says before it is ever a mechanical modifier, or it becomes the hidden-number trap.

INFORMATION, MEDIA & COMMUNICATION

Media Reputation
Emergent media identities — media darling, respected elder statesman, controversial firebrand, serial complainer, master of mind games — computed from a manager's accumulated press-stance history.
Socket: stance history is already logged (§2.2–2.3) and the Records Ledger keeps the long memory; the metric can be computed retrospectively whenever wanted, reconstructing nothing.
Waits for: a deliberate decision that the world is ready for it; explicitly "not yet."

Tactical Identity
What sort of football a club actually plays — Wide + Possession + Back Three feeling different from Central + Direct + Two Strikers. Not stronger, different. One of the things people grow attached to is being known for a way of playing, not only for winning.
Socket: it already emerges, with no new mechanic, from Tactical Familiarity, Cohesion, formation family, style and route to goal. The Information layer would only name and narrate it — a club's identity as something the news layer and the Records Ledger recognise.
Waits for: nothing mechanical — emergence from Familiarity is probably enough. If ever built, it is narrative, never a stat, in keeping with the narrative-before-mechanics discipline.

Perceived Balance
The metagame runs on belief, not equations: a formation family can be mathematically balanced and still die if managers collectively decide it is weak. Perceived balance matters almost as much as actual balance, and managers act on beliefs, not equations.
Socket: it links the Match Engine's ecosystem-usage target (Appendix D.11) to the Information layer, where belief is formed — what managers see, read and conclude. A candidate sentence for D.11, but fundamentally an information-flow phenomenon.
Waits for: a decision on where it lives; flagged because the calibration target it serves is already behavioural, so the belief that drives behaviour belongs in scope.

CANDIDATE CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENTS

Unlike the rest of this register, these are not features but principles already doing work locally that may deserve elevation to named constitutional status. The constitutional layer is frozen, so each would go through a deliberate amendment, never a quiet edit.

No Hidden Attribute System ("the game knows less than the real world")
The broad principle behind the Single-Rating Constraint and the three-archetype lean cap: no hidden attribute system may emerge through the accumulation of reasonable-sounding exceptions. Set-piece specialist, pressing specialist, target man, deep playmaker — each is defensible alone, and together they rebuild Football Manager's attribute screen without admitting it. That the game deliberately knows less than the real world is a strength, not a gap.
Home: the Player Rating Constitution, whose single-Ability model it defends, or the World Constitution as a project-wide rule.
Status: implicit in Player Rating, explicit but local in Appendix A; a clean one-principle amendment if elevated.

Football Is A Game Of Trade-Offs
Every tactical gain creates an exposure elsewhere — the generative rule beneath No Universal Solution, and the law the Appendix A matchup matrix is already built under.
Home: the Match Engine Constitution, as a named principle (a v0.7 amendment).
Status: currently Appendix A's binding design law; elevation would name at constitutional level what the matrix already enforces.
